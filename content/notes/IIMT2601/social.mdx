Social networks addresses the fundamental need to connect with the world. They have **strong network effects**, and **high switching cost**.

<Block variant="knBox" title="Social networks vs. social media">
Fundamentally, Social networks mainly encourage users to **connect with each other**. Social media mainly encourage users to **share content**.

    - Social networks: Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter
    - Social media: Instagram, TikTok, YouTube
</Block>

People spend hours on average per day on social media, which makes it a great place for advertisers to reach their target audience.

## Social marketing

The key point in social marketing is identifying customers, understanding their behavior, and measuring results. After creating content, it is important to **engage** with the audience. This can be done by asking questions, and responding to comments.
Marketers can use analytic tools to measure the effectiveness of their social media campaigns.



| **Advantages** | **Disadvantages** |
| :--- | ---: |
| Higher reach | Negative feedback |
| Better user tracking | Mistakes spread fast |
| Target audience | Biased feedback |
| User engagement |  |





<Block variant="definition" title="Metrics">
Indicating brand awareness:

    - Cost per impressions (CPI / CPM): The cost for displaying an ad to 1000 people
Indicating customer retention:

    - Customer retention rate: The percentage of customers that return
    - Average revenue per purchase
    - Average units per purchase
Indicating lead (interest) generation:

    - Click-through rate (CTR): The percentage of people who click on an ad
    - Conversion rate (CR): The percentage of people who take a desired action
    - Cost per Click (CPC)
    - Cost per Acquisition (CPA): The cost to acquire a customer
</Block>
